About Me

I will complete my PhD in Sociology next spring at Indiana University, where I have also earned an M.S. in Applied Statistics. In addition to quantitative methods, my substantive interests include immigration, race and ethnicity, politics, religion, and gender. My dissertation, funded by the NSF and a grant from the Global Religion Research Initiative, centers on immigration and identity processes. I investigate the emergence of a shared group identity in adolescence for Muslim descendants of immigrants in Europe, and I consider how this shared group identity can be used, in turn, for political engagement and incorporation in adulthood. I am also working on a project investigating if young men and women children of immigrants in the US follow different paths in identity formation between early adolescence and young adulthood.
